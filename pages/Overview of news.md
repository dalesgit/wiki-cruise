- Few Americans, if any, had ever heard of John Eastman before the events of [January 6, 2021](https://substack.com/redirect/9d005648-d3f9-4c32-930c-1623009cfe05?j=eyJ1IjoiMW91ejZvIn0.xCUUX-G1mqOe_p0uPEybRD6sQoY3OtX97OlEZbEJxWg). He was, in many ways, an unknown, pulled by the events and the aftermath of January 6 into the riptides of American history.
- A handful of analysts and investigators tracing the contours of the American far right, however, knew of Eastman‚Äôs work long before the events of January 6, and of the kind of churning, burning illiberalism that brought him into Trump‚Äôs orbit. One of those was Katherine Stewart, whose new book, _[Money, Lies, and God](https://substack.com/redirect/6885815a-1322-4282-b140-2119cbec619a?j=eyJ1IjoiMW91ejZvIn0.xCUUX-G1mqOe_p0uPEybRD6sQoY3OtX97OlEZbEJxWg),_ threads the diffuse movements that have congealed into the authoritarianism at the core of Trumpism.
- Stewart first intersected with Eastman not in the aftermath of Trump‚Äôs failed insurrection but in 2019, when she tracked him to a tony, rarefied conclave in, of all places, Verona, Italy. Eastman was speaking at the annual conference of the World Congress of Families‚Äîa far-right, Russian-American organization dedicated to the abolition of abortion and the buttressing of so-called ‚Äútraditional values‚Äù‚Äîregaling his audience with tales of ‚Äúhow secularism, liberalism, and gender confusion are destroying everything good in the world.‚Äù The speech, as Stewart writes, revealed Eastman to be ‚Äúa committed ally of the theocrats.‚Äù But there was also ‚Äúsome foundation other than biblical literalism,‚Äù Stewart sensed, propelling him and cementing the kinds of illiberal alliances crucial to Trump‚Äôs reign.
- The search for the broader foundations of Eastman‚Äôs and his allies‚Äô beliefs is at the center of _Money, Lies, and God_, the follow-up to Stewart‚Äôs critically acclaimed 2020 _[The Power Worshippers](https://substack.com/redirect/8d2699f7-d035-4acf-800b-0c6e06f67abf?j=eyJ1IjoiMW91ejZvIn0.xCUUX-G1mqOe_p0uPEybRD6sQoY3OtX97OlEZbEJxWg)._ That book explored the dogmatism at the core of the American religious right, and how such forces drew American evangelicals further and further into the illiberal abyss. It was, at the time, a signal flare of just how willingly such professedly godly groups could embrace a figure like Trump.
- Stewart‚Äôs newest book is a continuation of that theme. But after the events of January 6, it was clear that the right had changed. This movement‚Äîwith all the ideological allies, deep-pocketed donors, and misogynists massing around Eastman, joining him in this antidemocratic fight‚Äîpresents a new face of the American far right. And it is that edifice that Stewart masterfully details in her new book, explaining not only why these pro-Trump forces have already attempted to drive a stake through the heart of American democracy, but how they will try to do so once again.

- Part on-the-ground conversations, part sociological analysis, _Money, Lies, and God_ is a convincing tour of the mutually reinforcing elements of American reaction:
- > ‚Äúapostles‚Äù of Jesus, atheist billionaires, reactionary Catholic theologians, pseudo-Platonic intellectuals, woman-hating opponents of ‚Äúthe gynocracy,‚Äù high-powered evangelical networkers, Jewish devotees of Ayn Rand, pronatalists preoccupied with a dearth of (white) babies, COVID truthers, and battalions of ‚Äúspirit warriors‚Äù who appear to be inventing a new style of religion even as they set about undermining democracy at its foundations.
- Trumpism is hardly one thing, even if it is overly reliant on Trump himself. The ‚ÄúFunders‚Äù are those within the billionaire class bankrolling the forces below them, using access to effectively bottomless pots of wealth to guide American politics. Leo serves as the chair of CRC Advisors, which, as Stewart writes, ‚Äúdirects over $1 billion in right-wing funding toward reactionary causes.‚Äù Nor is Leo alone. There is Carl Anderson, head of the Knights of Columbus, who helps bankroll think tanks and media outlets. All of them together have access to, and help direct, a greater pile of money than anything hard-right forces in America have ever known. ‚ÄúThe decisive development in the first decades of the 21st century was not the alliance between Team Money and Team God but the simple fact that, thanks to escalating inequality, the big money got a lot bigger,‚Äù Stewart notes. ‚ÄúWhat was new was the number of zeroes in the checks‚Äîand the extremism of the thinking guiding the money people.‚Äù
- Elsewhere, the ‚ÄúPower Players‚Äù Stewart identifies are those just underneath the ‚ÄúFunders,‚Äù helping direct some of the ocean of money flooding pro-Trump, pro-authoritarian movements.
